How do scalp oils make hair healthier?

Many scalp oils are used as a pre-wash treatments, first massaged into the scalp to loosen debris, then shampooed out. After all, a healthy scalp sprouts fuller hair. Celebrity hair stylist and founder of Act+Acre, Helen Reavey, cites follicle-clogging buildup as a common cause of weakened strands. “Dullness, brittleness, or a lack of volume can start at the root,” she says.

What's the best method for using scalp oils?

When it comes to hair oils, these strand-centric formulas are used primarily to protect the hair and dial up shine. Most versions are also surprisingly lightweight, helping to make locks lustrous but not lank. For best results, these blends are usually applied to clean, damp hair as you would a leave-in conditioner.
