In recent years, technology has changed the way that we acquire, store, and listen to music. In the late 90s, the first MP3 players hit the shelves, eventually rendering CDs, tapes, and vinyl records almost obsolete. Of course, these improvements had a direct effect on DJ equipment, techniques, and abilities.

Modern DJ equipment is predominantly focused on digital forms of music, and the trusty turntable has been replaced with a simple laptop and controller. This means that transporting your equipment and music collection to and from gigs has never been easier, and while some may lament the old-school days, most will agree that the benefits of using digital audio equipment, and the range of functions it provides, far outweigh the nostalgia.

Here is the best DJ equipment available right now.

FAQ

Q: What Is the Difference Between a Controller and a Mixer?

A controller is a piece of hardware that is designed to interact with specialized DJ software, such as Serato, or Rekordbox. This software is used to play digital forms of music like MP3 or m4a and can change the tone, key, and beats per minute (BPM) of each track.

Controllers vary in functionality and may include built-in turntables for manually mixing two songs together, while the most complex models will invariably include features to add effects or otherwise modulate the track.

A mixer, in its most generic form, requires audio input from an external source such as a turntable or CD deck. Modern mixers, however, are something of a hybrid and can accept both digital and analog inputs, allowing skilled DJs to mix different tracks from a variety of sources.

Q: Can I DJ Tracks Directly From the Internet?

It is possible nowadays to DJ without having to download every track. Several DJ software services, including Serato DJ Pro, now have the ability to play tracks directly from a music streaming site, such as Beatport and SoundCloud. You will, however, need to ensure you have a reliable internet connection, as you could easily find yourself without music mid-set should the Wi-Fi stop working.

In addition, if you are playing in commercial venues there could be issues with copyright or licensing regulations. For peace of mind, the best option is to download your music in advance through a reputable source.

Q: Is It Possible to DJ Digital Tracks Without Using a Laptop?

Yes, it is. There are several high-quality all-in-one controllers available that come preloaded with the necessary software and have built-in screens for selecting tracks and organizing your music collection.

However, you will still need to have these stored on a memory stick or SD card. Standalone systems tend to be less popular as they can be quite bulky and somewhat more expensive than a standard controller.
