17 Best Luxury Outdoor Furniture Stores in 2024

Just as the perfect luxury couch or accent chair can completely transform your living room, the best luxury outdoor furniture can turn a barren patio into the ultimate open-air oasis. Not only does today's vast assortment make it easy to create a space that reflects your personal style, but buying high-end can also help ensure you get a better return on your investment.

"The outdoor category has exploded, and you can find outdoor furniture in pretty much every style and silhouette these days," Heather Goerzen, Havenly's lead designer, tells BAZAAR. "I think the key differences come down to quality and materials. The more inexpensive options are typically not as durable and may not last more than a summer or two, whereas something crafted from 100 percent wrought iron, teak wood, or even ultra-sturdy resin or plastic will last longer and better withstand the elements."

Of course, not all luxury furniture is made equal. But thankfully, there are a range of brands and retailers committed to producing high-quality, beautiful designs—even at more accessible price points. Whether you're starting from scratch or simply need a few luxury home decor accents, we found the 17 best luxury outdoor furniture stores to shop for every aesthetic.

What to look for when shopping for outdoor furniture

First and foremost, Goerzen recommends prioritizing options made with durable materials that can withstand the elements. "Quality and practicality are incredibly important when buying outdoor furniture, and if you don’t invest in something durable, you’ll be buying new pieces every few years," she notes. "Paying attention to the materials, especially when shopping online, is a good way to ensure your furniture is actually weather-resistant and outdoor-friendly. Opt for Sunbrella performance fabrics, wrought iron, teak wood, and always buy outdoor covers for each piece to keep them in good shape."

Naturally, size and aesthetics matter, too. "Silhouette and style-wise, I suggest going with pieces you’d also like indoors, or pieces that don’t scream 'patio'," says Goerzen. "There are so many cool, sculptural, modern items that feel just as unique and elevated as indoor pieces!"

"Wrought iron and natural materials, like wicker and teak, or even synthetic/faux wicker are not only high-quality and weather-resistant but also offer a mix of textures," Goerzen adds.
